# Local Guide - Hyderabad, Telangana Context

## Telugu Slang & Local Terms
- **"Babu"** - Casual way to address someone, like "dude" or "bro"
- **"Mama"** - Uncle, but used casually for any older guy
- **"Akka"** - Sister, used respectfully for any woman
- **"Anna"** - Brother, used for any guy older than you
- **"Enti ra"** - "What's up?" or "What happened?"
- **"Bagundi"** - "It's good" or "Nice"
- **"Chaala bagundi"** - "Very good" or "Excellent"
- **"Teesko"** - "Take it" (very common in Hyderabadi Hindi)
- **"Nakko"** - "I don't want" (mix of Telugu/Hindi)
- **"Kya scene hai"** - "What's the situation?" (Hyderabadi Hindi)
- **"Bindaas"** - "No worries" or "Cool"
- **"Potti"** - Girl (casual, not offensive)
- **"Abbai"** - Boy/Guy
- **"Arre yaar"** - "Come on, friend"
- **"Pakka"** - "Definitely" or "For sure"
- **"Tension lene ka nahi"** - "Don't take tension" (don't worry)

## Street Food & Local Eats

### Iconic Hyderabadi Dishes
- **Hyderabadi Biryani** - Dum-cooked rice with meat, served with raita and shorba
- **Haleem** - Slow-cooked lentil and meat stew, especially during Ramadan
- **Nihari** - Spicy meat curry, traditionally eaten for breakfast
- **Paya** - Trotters curry, weekend breakfast favorite
- **Qubani ka Meetha** - Apricot dessert with cream
- **Double ka Meetha** - Bread pudding with nuts and cream
- **Osmania Biscuits** - Sweet, salty biscuits with Irani chai

### Popular Street Food Spots
- **Charminar Area** - Historic food hub, best for traditional items
- **Moazzam Jahi Market** - Famous for fruits and street snacks
- **Abids** - Central location, good for evening snacks
- **Secunderabad Station** - Platform food, especially South Indian
- **Jubilee Hills** - Upscale street food, fusion items
- **Banjara Hills** - Modern food courts and cafes
- **Old City** - Authentic Hyderabadi cuisine, haleem during Ramadan
- **Kondapur** - IT crowd favorites, late-night options

### Street Food Items
- **Dosa** - South Indian crepe, available everywhere
- **Idli-Sambar** - Steamed rice cakes with lentil curry
- **Vada Pav** - Maharashtrian burger (popular in IT areas)
- **Pani Puri** - Water-filled crispy shells
- **Bhel Puri** - Puffed rice snack mix
- **Mirchi Bajji** - Stuffed chili fritters
- **Punugulu** - Fried lentil balls (Andhra special)
- **Pesarattu** - Green gram dosa
- **Keema Samosa** - Meat-filled pastries
- **Irani Chai** - Strong tea with biscuits

## Traffic Patterns & Transportation

### Peak Hours
- **Morning Rush** - 8:30-10:30 AM (office timings)
- **Evening Rush** - 6:00-8:30 PM (extended due to IT sector)
- **School Hours** - 7:30-8:30 AM, 3:30-4:30 PM
- **Weekend Rush** - Saturday evenings to malls and restaurants

### Major Traffic Corridors
- **Outer Ring Road (ORR)** - Connects IT hubs, heavy during office hours
- **Mehdipatnam to Jubilee Hills** - Always congested
- **Secunderabad to Banjara Hills** - Major north-south route
- **Charminar to Falaknuma** - Old city traffic, narrow roads
- **Kondapur to Gachibowli** - IT corridor, peak hour nightmare
- **Begumpet to Somajiguda** - Central business district route

### Area-wise Traffic Patterns
- **HITEC City/Gachibowli** - 9 AM and 7 PM IT crowd surge
- **Banjara Hills** - Evening restaurant and shopping traffic
- **Secunderabad** - Railway station area, always busy
- **Abids/Nampally** - Government offices, 10 AM-5 PM busy
- **Old City** - Friday prayers cause major jams around Charminar
- **Jubilee Hills** - Weekend party destination, Saturday night traffic

### Transportation Options
- **TSRTC Buses** - Extensive network, crowded during peak hours
- **Metro Rail** - Limited routes but expanding, connects major areas
- **Auto Rickshaws** - Everywhere, negotiate fare or use meter
- **Ola/Uber** - Widely available, surge pricing during peak hours
- **Bike Taxis** - Rapido popular for short distances
- **Local Trains** - Limited suburban network

## Cultural Context & Habits

### Language Mix
- **Telugu** - Primary local language, everyone understands
- **Hindi** - Widely spoken, especially in business areas
- **Urdu** - Common in Old City, mixed with Hindi
- **English** - IT sector and educated youth
- **Hyderabadi Hindi** - Unique dialect mixing Hindi, Urdu, Telugu

### Food Timings & Habits
- **Breakfast** - 7-9 AM, usually South Indian or parathas
- **Lunch** - 12:30-2 PM, rice-based meals common
- **Evening Snacks** - 4-6 PM, chai with biscuits or street food
- **Dinner** - 8-10 PM, often biryani or North Indian
- **Late Night** - 10 PM-12 AM, biryani joints stay open
- **Weekend Breakfast** - Paya, nihari, or special items until noon

### Social Habits
- **Chai Culture** - Multiple tea breaks, Irani chai preferred
- **Family Dining** - Restaurants accommodate large groups
- **Ramadan** - Haleem everywhere, iftar timings affect traffic
- **Wedding Season** - October-February, affects weekend traffic
- **Cricket Matches** - India games empty the streets
- **Movie Culture** - Telugu films very popular, first-day shows crowded

### Weather Impact
- **Summer** - March-June, extreme heat affects outdoor dining
- **Monsoon** - July-September, waterlogging in low-lying areas
- **Winter** - October-February, pleasant weather, more outdoor activities
- **Festival Season** - Ganesh Chaturthi, Diwali cause traffic diversions

## Neighborhoods & Areas

### IT Corridor
- **HITEC City** - Major IT hub, expensive restaurants
- **Gachibowli** - IT companies, modern infrastructure
- **Kondapur** - IT residential area, good food options
- **Madhapur** - Mixed IT and residential, traffic bottleneck

### Central Areas
- **Banjara Hills** - Upscale area, fine dining, pubs
- **Jubilee Hills** - Elite residential, expensive shopping
- **Somajiguda** - Central business district
- **Abids** - Shopping area, government offices nearby

### Traditional Areas
- **Old City/Charminar** - Historic area, authentic Hyderabadi food
- **Secunderabad** - Twin city, railway hub, cantonment area
- **Begumpet** - Airport area, business hotels
- **Nampally** - Government offices, budget accommodations

### Residential Areas
- **Kukatpally** - Middle-class suburb, good connectivity
- **Miyapur** - Growing residential area, metro connectivity
- **Uppal** - Eastern suburb, affordable housing
- **LB Nagar** - Southern suburb, IT professionals area
